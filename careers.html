Anthropic is an AI safety company building reliable, interpretable, and steerable AI systems. Their team includes researchers, engineers, policy experts, and business leaders united by a commitment to beneficial AI — pioneering some of the most consequential technology of our time.
The Opportunity
Model Context Protocol (MCP) is Anthropic's open standard for connecting AI models to external tools, data sources, and services. Since its launch in November 2024, the developer community has built hundreds of integrations on top of MCP — and adoption is accelerating. As a Software Engineer on the MCP team, you will help define and build the protocol that sits at the center of the agentic AI ecosystem, working in public alongside a vibrant open source community.
What You'll Do
  • Design and implement core MCP protocol features in collaboration with the open source community
  • Build and maintain SDKs and tooling that make MCP easy to adopt across languages and frameworks
  • Support external developers and internal teams building on MCP — through code, docs, and direct engagement
  • Develop showcase implementations that demonstrate MCP's capabilities and set quality standards for the ecosystem
  • Write clear, comprehensive technical documentation that helps developers succeed quickly
  • Participate in code reviews and provide mentorship to community contributors
  • Help shape the long-term technical direction of the protocol while ensuring backward compatibility
  • Drive technical discussions in public channels, GitHub issues, and community forums
Required Qualifications

  • Strong proficiency in TypeScript/JavaScript and Python
  • Experience building and maintaining open source projects or developer-facing tools

  • Excellent written communication skills — you can explain complex systems clearly
  • Comfortable working asynchronously in a highly collaborative, public-facing environment
Preferred Qualifications

  • Familiarity with AI/LLM application development and agentic workflows

  • Track record of community leadership in open source ecosystems
